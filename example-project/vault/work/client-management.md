# Client management cadence

How I keep relationships steady once a client is past kickoff.

## Weekly check-in (every client, every week)

- The same templated email goes out every Friday.
- Even a "nothing material to report" week gets one — predictability is
  half the value.
- See `async-client-comms` for the structure.

## Quarterly review

A 30-45 minute call at the end of each quarter:

- What went well, what didn't, what would the client change.
- Forecast next quarter's likely scope.
- Confirm hours/budget alignment for the next 90 days.

I keep a brief summary of each review in the client folder. Trends across
two or three quarters tell me whether to invest more in a relationship or
to ease out.

## Ad-hoc

- Same-day reply on anything that looks like an outage or a deadline.
- Otherwise, batched replies twice a day to protect deep work.
- Calls only by appointment; never on demand.

## Boundary rules

- No work on weekends except for emergencies, defined narrowly.
- Out-of-office is announced two weeks ahead and means out of office.
- All decisions captured in writing, even if the conversation was a call.

## Notes from the past quarter

- The Friday updates are doing what they're supposed to do — Maria stopped
  pinging mid-week the moment they started arriving reliably.
- Next quarter: try a fortnightly written reflection separate from the
  client update, just for me. Aim is to spot patterns earlier.
