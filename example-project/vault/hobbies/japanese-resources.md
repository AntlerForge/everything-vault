# Japanese resources

What I actually use, sorted by how often I open it.

## Daily

- **Anki** — two decks. A core vocab deck (working through the most-common
  N4 words), and a kanji deck where each card has a personal mnemonic on
  the back.
- **A reading practice app** for graded readers in the morning.

## Weekly

- **Genki II textbook + workbook** — the second of the two main Genki
  textbooks, the standard intro pair. Currently on chapter 14.
- **NHK Easy News** — Saturday morning reading. Short articles with furigana.
- **Nihongo con Teppei** podcast — long-running beginner-to-intermediate
  podcast, used for Sunday listening.

## Monthly-ish

- **Bunpro** — for grammar drilling when I notice a point I haven't
  internalised. Not a daily habit; works better as a checkup tool.
- **Tadoku graded readers** — free PDFs at various levels. Good for
  building reading stamina.

## Tried and dropped

- A famous immersion-first podcast — too long-form for current commute time.
- Watching anime with Japanese subtitles — flagged as "study" but actually
  collapsed into entertainment, so it didn't count as practice.
