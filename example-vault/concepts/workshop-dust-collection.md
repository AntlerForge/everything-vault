# Strategic dust collection in a small workshop

In a small home workshop you can't reasonably plumb cyclone ducting to every
station. A more useful framing: most of the harmful dust comes from a small
number of heavy-cut tools, and capture should be sized to their actual use
profile.

## The principle

Identify the **two or three loudest dust sources** by hours-of-use × particle
size, and treat those properly with a mobile extractor on a flexible 100mm
hose. Treat everything else with a HEPA shop vac and brush attachments.
Don't try to centralise.

## Why this beats "central system"

- A central system in a 4×3m space steals floor space and only really helps
  if all stations are used in the same session.
- Hose drag at the saw and planer beats no extraction everywhere else.
- A respirator covers the long tail of "I just need to plane one piece."

## Implications for the bench build

The new bench should sit so the planer-thicknesser station is the closest
point to the extractor outlet. That ten-second routing decision matters
more than any extra ducting.

## Open question

Worth a separate downdraft sanding station, or is shop-vac-on-the-port good
enough? Defer until after the bench is in.
