# Workshop setup

A back-garden workshop in a converted shed. 4.0m × 3.0m, insulated, single
double-glazed window, single 13A circuit on its own consumer unit module.

## Power tools (stationary)

- Bandsaw — 14", on a mobile base.
- Planer-thicknesser — 10", combo unit.
- Router table — small, shop-built.

## Power tools (handheld)

- Track saw + 1.4m and 0.7m tracks.
- Two routers (1/2" and 1/4").
- Random orbit sander.
- Cordless drill / driver / impact set.

## Hand tools

- Hand planes: #4, #5, low-angle block, shoulder plane.
- Chisels: bevel-edged set 6-25mm, plus a couple of mortise chisels.
- Saws: tenon, dovetail, crosscut.
- Marking and measuring: combination square, marking gauge, mortise gauge,
  marking knife.

## Storage

- Wall-mounted French cleat system on the long wall.
- Open shelving over the bench area for jigs and consumables.
- Lumber rack on the back wall (vertical storage, easier on a small floor).

## Lighting

Twin 6ft LED battens overhead, plus task lighting at the bench and the
bandsaw.

## Dust extraction

See `workshop-dust-collection`. Mobile extractor + HEPA shop vac, no central
ducting.
