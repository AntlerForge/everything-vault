# Three favourite routes

All routes start and end at the flat. Distances and times are rough.

## Route 1 — River loop

A short flat loop along the river path, out to a small village, back via
the canal towpath. About 18 km, 60-75 minutes at conversational pace.

Used as: Tuesday/Friday default, errand rides, recovery days.

## Route 2 — Downs and out

Climbs up to one of the city's open commons, then heads out through quiet
suburban roads onto a B-road heading south-west. Returns on the same B-road
or via a slightly longer loop on country lanes. Roughly 35 km, 90-120
minutes. Two real climbs.

Used as: midweek longer ride when there's daylight, or a Saturday short.

## Route 3 — Country day

The long Saturday ride. Heads out of the city through quieter back streets,
joins the long-distance cycle path, climbs up onto the surrounding
countryside, loops a familiar circuit of villages, and comes back via a
different path. 70-90 km depending on which loop, 3-4 hours.

Used as: long Saturday rides, building endurance for any future cycling
holiday.

## Tips for myself

- Route 3 is no good if it's been raining hard — the cycle path floods at
  two reliable spots.
- Carry an extra tube on Route 3. The shops are sparse on the loop.
- Pack a real lunch, not just a gel.
