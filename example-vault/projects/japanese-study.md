# Japanese study — toward N4

## Where I am

JLPT N5 passed February 2026 (140/180). Now working through Genki II, on
chapter 14. N4 exam booked for July 2026.

## Weekly schedule

| Day        | Block             | Activity                                |
|------------|-------------------|-----------------------------------------|
| Mon-Fri    | 20 min morning    | Anki review (vocab + kanji decks)       |
| Tue, Thu   | 45 min evening    | Genki textbook chapter / workbook       |
| Saturday   | 60 min            | Reading practice (NHK Easy, graded readers) |
| Sunday     | 30 min            | Listening (Nihongo con Teppei)          |

Roughly 5-6 hours a week. Enough to be steady, not enough to be fast.

## Kanji approach

I use the mnemonics method described in `kanji-mnemonics-method` — every
new kanji gets a one-line visual story tied to its components. About 15 new
kanji a week. Retention with mnemonics has been visibly better than the
brute-force flashcards I used for the first hundred.
